Powders and granules :

Solid dosage form , mixture of drug and excipient that are dried and micronized into fine particles according to the composition :
1- Complex two or more active ingredient
2- Simple powder one active ingredient

To the administration route :-

1- External topical or dusting powder
2- Internal oral powder

Dusting powder

Complex powder , externally used only Spreading on the body surface are

Experiment: Talc powder

RX :
Purified talc 500 gm
Starch 250 gm
Zinc oxide 250 gm

Method of preparation :

1- Weight the ( talc , starch , zinc oxide ) .
2- Use a sieve to sift each of them .
3- Add ZnO then starch then purified talc on mortar
4- Titration .

Uses

1- Dusting powder
2- Lubricant
3- Anti itching
4- Moisture absorbent

( Red label )

NOTE

Generic name of talc ( Hydrated magnesium silicate ).

Source of talc ( Talc rock ) .

Effervescent powder :

Granular powder that used internal with amount of water while the granules are effervescing ( CO2 gas ) drink the solution .

Effervescent powder assessments:

1- The time needs to start effervescing
2- Purity of the solution
3- Time needs to finish effervescing
4- Co2 volume release
